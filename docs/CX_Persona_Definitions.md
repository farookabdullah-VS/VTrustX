# CX Persona Attribute List

This document outlines the standard attributes and data points required to develop comprehensive Customer Experience (CX) personas across various industries.

## 1. Core Profile (Universal)
These attributes form the foundation of any persona, regardless of industry.

### Identity
- **Persona Name**: catchy moniker (e.g., "Efficient Emily", "Budget Bob").
- **Avatar/Image**: Visual representation.
- **Quote/Motto**: A single sentence summarizing their mindset.
- **Bio**: A brief backstory.

### Demographics
- **Age Range**: e.g., 25-34, 45-55.
- **Gender**: Male, Female, Non-binary/Other.
- **Location**: Urban, Suburban, Rural; Specific Region.
- **Education Level**: High School, Undergraduate, Graduate, PhD.
- **Income Level**: Annual household or individual income bracket.
- **Family Status**: Single, Married, Parents, Empty Nesters.

### Professional
- **Job Title**: Current role.
- **Industry**: Sector they work in.
- **Sensitivities**: Time-poor, Budget-constrained, Risk-averse.

## 2. Psychographics & Behavior
Understanding *why* they act the way they do.

### Drivers
- **Core Values**: Sustainability, Convenience, Status, Reliability, Innovation.
- **Goals**: What are they trying to achieve? (e.g., "Automate daily tasks", "Find best value").
- **Motivations**: What prompts them to act? (e.g., Fear of missing out, Desire for efficiency).

### Pain Points
- **Frustrations**: What annoys them about current solutions?
- **Barriers**: What stops them from converting? (Price, Complexity, Trust).
- **Fears**: What are they afraid of happening? (Data loss, Bad quality).

### CX Preferences
- **Preferred Channels**: Email, SMS, Phone, In-App Chat, Social Media.
- **Tech Savviness**: Low (needs help), Medium, High (power user).
- **Brand Loyalty**: Switcher (price focused), Loyalist (brand focused), Passive.
- **Response to Marketing**: Loves offers, Ignores ads, Researches heavily.

## 3. Industry-Specific Attributes

### A. Retail & E-Commerce
- **Shopping Frequency**: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Seasonal.
- **Buying Journey**: Impulse buyer vs. Extensive researcher.
- **Platform Preference**: Mobile App, Desktop Web, In-Store, Social Commerce.
- **Discount Sensitivity**: Full-price buyer vs. Coupon hunter.
- **Returns Behavior**: Frequent returner vs. Keep-what-you-buy.
- **Product Categories**: Fashion, Electronics, Home Goods, Groceries.

### B. Healthcare (Patient Experience)
- **Health Goal**: Wellness/Prevention, Chronic Management, Acute Treatment.
- **Insurance Status**: Private, Public/Medicare, Uninsured.
- **Digital Health Adoption**: Telehealth user, Wearable tracker user, Skeptic.
- **Caregiver Status**: Patient is self vs. Caregiver for child/parent.
- **Trust Factor**: Rely on Doctor's word vs. Self-researcher (Dr. Google).

### C. Financial Services (Banking & Fintech)
- **Financial Stage**: Student, accumulator, Pre-retiree, Retiree.
- **Risk Tolerance**: Low (Savings/CDs), Medium, High (Crypto/Stocks).
- **Banking Habit**: Digital-only (Neobank), Hybrid, Branch-dependent.
- **Primary Need**: Debt management, Wealth growth, Daily transaction ease.
- **Advisory Needs**: DIY Investor vs. Managed Service seeker.

### D. B2B / SaaS (Software as a Service)
- **Buying Role**: Decision Maker (Signer), Influencer, End User, Gatekeeper.
- **Company Size**: Startup (<50), SME (50-500), Enterprise (500+).
- **Budget Authority**: <$1k, $1k-$10k, $10k+, Unlimited.
- **Adoption Speed**: Early Adopter vs. Laggard.
- **Support Needs**: Self-serve docs, Dedicated Account Manager, Community support.

### E. Travel & Hospitality
- **Travel Purpose**: Business, Leisure, Family, "Bleisure" (Business+Leisure).
- **Booking Method**: Direct (Brand site), OTA (Expedia/Booking), Travel Agent.
- **Frequency**: Frequent Flyer, Once-a-year vacationer.
- **Accommodation Style**: Luxury, Budget/Hostel, Boutique, Airbnb/Rental.
- **Loyalty Program Status**: Elite, Member, None.

### F. Real Estate
- **Buyer Type**: First-time buyer, Investor, Upsizer, Downsizer.
- **Timeline**: Immediate, 3-6 months, Long-term browsing.
- **Financing**: Cash buyer, Pre-approved mortgage, Needs assistance.
- **Priorities**: Location, School District, ROI, Turn-key condition.

## 4. Journey Map Data Points
For each persona, you should map their experience across:
1.  **Awareness**: How do they find you?
2.  **Consideration**: What do they compare you with?
3.  **Purchase**: How easy is the transaction?
4.  **Retention**: What keeps them coming back?
5.  **Advocacy**: Will they refer others?
